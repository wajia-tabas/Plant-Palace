Categories

Internally, plants are classified based on their vascular systems and reproductive methods, distinguishing between vascular and non-vascular plants, as well as seed-producing (angiosperms and gymnosperms) and spore-producing plants. Vascular plants have specialized tissues like xylem and phloem, while non-vascular plants lack these and rely on direct water absorption.

Externally, plants are classified based on visible traits like growth habits, structure, and environment, considering factors such as life cycle (annuals, biennials, perennials), growth form (herbs, shrubs, trees), and environmental adaptation (aquatic, desert, tropical). This classification highlights how plants are suited to specific conditions and habitats.

Succulents are low-maintenance plants with thick, water-storing leaves, perfect for adding greenery to any space. Their unique shapes and vibrant colors make them a popular choice for indoor and outdoor decor.

Flowering shrubs are versatile plants that produce beautiful, vibrant blooms, adding color and texture to gardens and landscapes. They require minimal care and can thrive in various climates, making them a popular choice for enhancing outdoor spaces.
